Gone Now by Bleachers

Album Reviews Bleachers
June 4th, 2018

In the past, frontman Jack Antonoff has described the sound of Bleachers as someone struggling with their thoughts alone in a room -- and that description is still just as accurate today as it was in 2014, when Bleachers' first album, Strange Desire, was released.

Although Strange Desire had its moments of external connection, like Rollercoaster and I Wanna Get Better (two of its most successful radio singles, by no coincidence), it was first and foremost a diaristic collection of internal thoughts and self-reflection. Gone Now is also innately diaristic and reflective, but now Antonoff is examining the people and events around him with the same intense focus that was directed at himself and his past in his earlier work.

MASSEDUCTION by St Vincent

Album Reviews St Vincent
June 6th, 2018

Annie Clark, the mastermind behind St Vincent, has never struggled with originality, creativity, or playing the guitar insanely well, and her latest release makes that more clear than ever.

MASSEDUCTION is my favorite St Vincent record because it is her most brutally honest, unapologetic work thus far. It's a beautiful and sad meditation on power, loss, and lust.

St Vincent by St Vincent

Album Reviews St Vincent
June 6th, 2018

This is the only St Vincent album that I don't have a firm opinion on yet -- which is funny because from what I've read this album was her most critically acclaimed.

To me it sounds very loud and noisy, to the point where it's difficult to hear what she's trying to say through all the toughness of her guitar riffs. I think that I need to give it some more time, because I trust that Annie Clark is saying something worth hearing beneath the heavy instumentation.
