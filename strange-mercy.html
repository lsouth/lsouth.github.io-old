Album Review: Strange Mercy by St Vincent

Album Reviews St Vincent
July 24th, 2018

This is my favorite St Vincent album, after about six months of being a fan of Annie Clark's music. The first time that I listened to this album all the way through was as I walked home from class at dusk through the pouring rain. That sounds like an exaggeration for narrative purposes, but I swear its true. Anyways, it was the perfect atmosphere in which to absorb all that Annie Clark has to say in Strange Mercy.

I read in an interview with some magazine that Annie secluded herself in an apartment in Seattle when she was making this record. She would wake up, eat breakfast, and do nothing but write and record for 12 hours each day. Strange Mercy reflects the monastic focus that went into its creation. The world of Strange Mercy is meticulously crafted and well organized, but there is a steady thrum of sadness and anguish beneath its silky veneer.
