Album Review: Melodrama by Lorde

Album Reviews Lorde
June 4th, 2018

I adore this album. When I first listened to it in June 2017, I remember thinking that I'd been living this album for the past six months without even realizing it. It's been on pretty much constant replay on my Spotify since it came out on June 15, 2017. Every song felt so familiar to me that I couldn't pick a favorite for months. But now I can say with confidence that my favorite song on Melodrama is Hard Feelings. The last verse is so sad and beautiful:

Three years, loved you every single day, made me weak, it was real for me
Now I'll fake it every single day til I don't need fantasy, til I feel you leave
But I still remember everything, how we'd drift buying groceries, how you'd dance for me
I'll start letting go of little things til I'm so far away from you, far away from you, yeah.

That said, I can see how this album could be seen as underwhelming to someone who didn't go through an experience similar to what Lorde discusses over and over in this album. It's definitely thematically very different from Pure Heroine. PH was very detached, and that gave it a certain cool feeling. Any chill that Lorde had when she was 16 is gone now -- which I support. Lorde is living her life, she's not just sitting on the sidelines making erudite observations about her surroundings. I think in an interview she said Pure Heroine was like watching people get drunk and Melodrama is about jumping into the fray and getting wasted yourself. So at least Lorde and I are on the same page here.
