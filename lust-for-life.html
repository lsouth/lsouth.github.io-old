Album Review: Lust For Life by Lana Del Rey

Album Reviews Lana Del Rey
December 22, 2018

I knew that Lust For Life would stand apart from the rest of Lana Del Rey's discography the first time I saw the album art. Lana stares directly at the camera, smiling widely in front of an old-fashioned truck. Every other Lana record has featured a picture of Lana staring into the camera with a serious, unsmiling expression, so I knew that it wasn't a coincidence that she chose to not showcase the same seriousness on this cover. I was right; this album is very different from other Lana Del Rey records, and I mean that in the best possible way. There is a deep-seated happiness present in this record. Lana seemed occasionally happy in 2014's Ultraviolence, but her happiness always seemed like a by-product of everything else in her life and her music rather than a full-fledged topic to sing about in its own right.

One of my favorite tracks is "In My Feelings". Lana calls out the "losers" she has a tendency to fall in love with, and begins the chorus with one of my favorite lines in this whole world:

Who's doper than this bitch?
Who's free-er than me?
You wanna make the switch, be my guest baby
I'm feeling all my fucking feelings

Lana has been criticized in the past for making anti-feminist music because her lyrics tend to revolve around deep, losing-yourself-for-a-man love. It feels like Lana is speaking to the music industry along with an ex-boyfriend in this song -- she's going to feel her fucking feelings whether you like it or not.
