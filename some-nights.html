Album Review: Some Nights by fun.

This is the second album released by the band fun. I must note that I truly hate the period at the end of their name. I'm glad Jack Antonoff learned his lesson and didn't name his next band Bleachers? or Bleachers' or something equally dumb. Anyways, Some Nights is a brilliant album. It feels so different from fun.'s previous album, Aim & Ignite, that I wouldn't have been surprised if I learned that the band had experienced a lot of member turnover between the two releases. But no, the band is still comprised of Nate Ruess (lead singer), Andrew Dost (piano and various other instruments, including, amazingly, the flugelhorn), and Jack Antonoff (guitar and various other instruments). Technically the band has been on hiatus since early 2015, but I have a feeling that fun period is forever done period. All credit to Vanessa Bayer for that joke.

Some Nights was produced by Jeff Bhasker, who until this album had worked exclusively with hip-hop artists. I think it was Bhasker's influence that made Some Nights feel slicker and more polished than fun.'s previous work. Luckily, there are several moments in this album that call back to Aim and Ignite's more earnest style and remind us that this is still the same band, just slightly more dressed up.
